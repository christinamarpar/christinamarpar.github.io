Notes on The Young Entrepreneur's Guide to Starting & Running a Business by Steve Mariotti

Create Your Business

  • Funding:

    • Angel investors = Private individuals
      • Investment typically b/w 100k and 500k
      • Angels typically seek a 10x return
      • Typically invest on an equity basis
      • Resources:
        • Angel List
        • Accion USA
        • Angel Capital Association
        • Association for Enterprise Opportunity
        • Opportunity Finance Network
    • Venture Capitalists = investors and companies specializing in high-potential entrepreneurial companies
      • VCs typically seek a 6x return over a five year period
      • VC-backed companies typically have projected sales of $25M+ within the first 5 years
      • Ideally: $50M within 5 years, growing at 30% to 50% per year, with pretax profit margins over 20%
    • SBICs provide equity financing and loans for small businesses
    • MESBICs (minority and women), chartered by the SBA, also provide debt and equity financing
    • Crowdfunding Platforms:
      • Kickstarter
      • Indiegogo
    • To approach a bank or investor, you will need a business plan including sales forecasts.

  • Business Plan Outline:

    • Executive Summary (1-2 pages)
    • Mission Statement (1-2 sentences)
    • Qualifications
    • Analysis of your competition
    • Your competitive advantages: What do you do better than the competition?
    • Short- and long-term growth goals
    • Challenges to your business
    • Marketing plan
    • Sales methods and estimates
    • Financial information and operations
    • Projected financial statements (income statement, balance sheet, cash flow statement)
    • Start-up investment
    • Financial ratio analysis
    • Exit strategy

Connecting with Customers:

  • Market Research
    • Market = individuals willing and able to purchase a particular product or service
    • Marketing = how you reach them
    • Market research = identify the group of customers most likely to buy your product or service
  • Market benefits, not features
    • Features of DuoLivre = clean, reliable interface
    • Benefits of DuoLivre =
      • *Immersion* and, ultimately, *Fluency* in your foreign language
      • Fun to use
      • Tailored to your specific interests
      • Not awkward or embarrassing (compared to a conversation you might have with a native speaker)
  • Competitive advantage:
    • DuoLivre is first-to-market
    • No other application allows you to read your favorite books, *in your foreign language* today, without studying a minute more.
